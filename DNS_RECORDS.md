# DNS Records for notch-tech.com

Add these TXT records to your DNS provider (e.g. Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap).

---

## SPF Record
Tells mail servers which hosts are allowed to send email on behalf of notch-tech.com.

| Type | Name | Value                                          | TTL  |
|------|------|------------------------------------------------|------|
| TXT  | @    | `v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all`          | 3600 |

> If you use a different email provider (Outlook, Brevo, etc.), replace `include:_spf.google.com`
> with the SPF record provided by your provider.

---

## DMARC Record
Instructs receiving mail servers on what to do with emails that fail SPF/DKIM checks.

| Type | Name    | Value                                                                   | TTL  |
|------|---------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------|------|
| TXT  | _dmarc  | `v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@notch-tech.com; pct=100`    | 3600 |

### DMARC Policy Values:
- `p=none` — Monitor only (no action taken). Use this first to test.
- `p=quarantine` — Suspicious emails go to spam. Recommended once SPF/DKIM is stable.
- `p=reject` — Strict. Unauthorized emails are rejected entirely.

> **Tip:** Start with `p=none`, check the reports at `dmarc@notch-tech.com` for 2–4 weeks,
> then upgrade to `p=quarantine` and finally `p=reject`.

---

## DKIM Record (optional but strongly recommended)
Generated by your email provider. Example for Google Workspace:

| Type | Name                        | Value                          | TTL  |
|------|-----------------------------|--------------------------------|------|
| TXT  | google._domainkey           | `v=DKIM1; k=rsa; p=<your-key>`| 3600 |

> Get your DKIM key from Google Admin Console → Apps → Google Workspace → Gmail → Authenticate email.
